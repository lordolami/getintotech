Risks â€” And How We Think About Them

We know the risks.
We have thought through them.

Risk 01

AI API cost at scale

Claude API is our biggest cost. At 100K active users doing 10 messages/day, that is ~$15,000/mo. Mitigation: Pro subscription caps AI usage. Free tier has a daily message limit. Schools AI usage is billed into the subscription. We model this carefully before every pricing decision.

Risk 02

Exam integrity

What if someone finds a way to cheat? Mitigation: Unique test cases per attempt make sharing useless. Tab-switching tracking. Employer-requested webcam proctoring. Certificate links to a live blockchain record. The whole system is designed around this.

Risk 03

Larger platforms copying features

What if Coursera or Udemy copies us? Mitigation: They cannot ship free. Their entire business model is charging learners. Flipping to free would destroy their revenue. We are structurally protected by being the only platform whose incentives are fully aligned with learners.

Risk 04

COPPA compliance (children's data)

Schools means children under 13. COPPA violations are serious. Mitigation: $50K of the raise is earmarked for legal and compliance. COPPA-compliant data handling is designed into the schools product from the start, not retrofitted later.

Risk 05

School sales cycle is long

Schools can take 6â€“12 months to make purchasing decisions. Mitigation: We target individual teachers first â€” they sign up schools. Teacher adoption converts to school contracts. We also target private schools, coding academies, and bootcamps with faster decision cycles first.

Risk 06

Solo founder execution risk

What if the founder burns out or gets stuck? Mitigation: The 164-section spec means no decision paralysis. Every engineering choice is already made. The build plan is 800 specific tasks. This is execution, not exploration. And funding means the founder can hire before it becomes a single point of failure.
